About Our Practice

Meet our team of dedicated mental health professionals and learn about our approach to therapy and counseling.

Our Story

Counseling Therapy was founded in 2005 with a simple mission: to provide accessible, high-quality mental health services to individuals from all walks of life. What began as a small practice with just three therapists has grown into a comprehensive mental health center with multiple locations and a diverse team of professionals.

Our founder, Dr. Emily Johnson, established Counseling Therapy after recognizing the need for a more holistic, client-centered approach to mental health care. She envisioned a practice where clients would feel safe, respected, and empowered to make positive changes in their lives.

Today, we continue to uphold Dr. Johnson's vision by combining evidence-based therapeutic techniques with compassionate care that acknowledges the unique experiences and needs of each client.

Our Approach to Therapy

Client-Centered Care

We believe that you are the expert on your own life. Our therapists serve as guides and allies in your healing journey, helping you tap into your innate wisdom and strengths. We create a collaborative therapeutic relationship where your voice is heard and respected.

Evidence-Based Practices

Our therapeutic approaches are grounded in research and proven to be effective. We integrate methods such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction, and other evidence-based modalities tailored to your specific needs.

Holistic Perspective

We recognize that mental health is influenced by many factors, including physical health, relationships, environment, and life experiences. Our therapists take a whole-person approach, considering how these various elements interact to affect your well-being.

Cultural Sensitivity

We honor the diverse backgrounds, identities, and perspectives of our clients. Our therapists are committed to ongoing cultural humility and providing care that respects and affirms all aspects of your identity.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

Therapy can be beneficial for anyone looking to gain insights, develop coping skills, or navigate life's challenges. If you're experiencing persistent distress, relationship difficulties, or simply want to enhance your personal growth, therapy might be a good option. We offer free 15-minute consultations to help you determine if our services are a good fit for your needs.

How long does therapy typically last?

The duration of therapy varies depending on your goals and circumstances. Some clients benefit from short-term therapy (8-12 sessions) focused on specific issues, while others prefer longer-term support for more complex concerns. We'll work with you to create a treatment plan tailored to your unique situation and regularly assess your progress.

Do you accept insurance?

Yes, we accept many major insurance plans. We also offer sliding scale fees for those without insurance coverage. Please contact our office for specific information about your insurance plan and coverage options.

What should I expect in my first therapy session?

Your initial session will focus on getting to know you and understanding your concerns. Your therapist will ask questions about your current situation, relevant history, and what you hope to achieve through therapy. This is also an opportunity for you to ask questions and determine if the therapist is a good match for your needs.
